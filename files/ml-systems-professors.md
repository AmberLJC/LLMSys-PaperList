## Profiles

- **Ion Stoica** (UC Berkeley) — [Google Scholar](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=vN-is70AAAAJ) — Professor of Computer Science at UC Berkeley and co-creator of foundational distributed systems including Apache Spark, Apache Mesos, and Ray, and co-founder of Databricks and Anyscale.
- **Matei Zaharia** (UC Berkeley) — [Google Scholar](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=I1EvjZsAAAAJ) — Associate Professor at UC Berkeley and co-founder/CTO of Databricks who created Apache Spark and contributed to MLflow, Delta Lake, and ColBERT.
- **Tianqi Chen** (CMU) — [Google Scholar](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=7nlvOMQAAAAJ) — Assistant Professor at Carnegie Mellon and NVIDIA Distinguished Engineer, creator of XGBoost, Apache TVM, and MXNet, and a leader in ML compilation and systems.
- **Song Han** (MIT) — [Google Scholar](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=E0iCaa4AAAAJ) — Associate Professor at MIT EECS known for efficient deep learning, including Deep Compression, hardware-aware NAS (Once-for-All), and LLM acceleration (SmoothQuant, AWQ, StreamingLLM).
- **Christopher Ré** (Stanford) — [Google Scholar](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=DnnCWN0AAAAJ) — Professor at Stanford and MacArthur Fellow working at the intersection of data management and ML systems, known for FlashAttention, state-space models (Mamba/S4), and weak supervision (Snorkel).
- **Luis Ceze** (University of Washington) — [Google Scholar](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=KzESVKwAAAAJ) — Professor at the University of Washington and co-founder/CEO of OctoAI, co-creator of Apache TVM and a pioneer in DNA data storage.
- **Joseph E. Gonzalez** (UC Berkeley) — [Google Scholar](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=B96GkdgAAAAJ) — Professor at UC Berkeley working on AI systems, whose group produced vLLM, Chatbot Arena, SGLang, Gorilla, and GraphX.
- **Zhihao Jia** (CMU) — [Google Scholar](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=0IWLFR4AAAAJ) — Assistant Professor at Carnegie Mellon focused on ML systems and automated parallelization, known for FlexFlow and work on distributed DNN training and serving.
- **Carlos Guestrin** (Stanford) — [Google Scholar](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=DpLFv4gAAAAJ) — Professor at Stanford known for scalable machine learning, GraphLab, XGBoost, and interpretable ML (LIME).
- **Eric P. Xing** (CMU / MBZUAI) — [Google Scholar](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=5pKTRxEAAAAJ) — Professor at CMU and President of MBZUAI, known for large-scale distributed ML systems including Petuum and the parameter server paradigm.
- **Onur Mutlu** (ETH Zurich) — [Google Scholar](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=7XyGUGkAAAAJ) — Professor of Computer Architecture at ETH Zurich leading the SAFARI group, known for memory systems research including RowHammer and processing-in-memory.
- **Mosharaf Chowdhury** (University of Michigan) — [Google Scholar](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=Dzh5C9EAAAAJ) — Associate Professor at the University of Michigan leading SymbioticLab, known for cluster scheduling, federated learning (FedScale), and the ML Energy Initiative.
- **Shivaram Venkataraman** (UW–Madison) — [Google Scholar](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=5LLV29oAAAAJ) — Associate Professor at UW–Madison working on systems for large-scale ML and data analytics, including distributed training and cluster resource management.
- **Ana Klimovic** (ETH Zurich) — [Google Scholar](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=i7jievkAAAAJ) — Assistant Professor at ETH Zurich leading the Efficient Architectures and Systems Lab, focused on cloud computing, serverless systems, and efficient ML data pipelines.
- **Tri Dao** (Princeton) — [Google Scholar](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=NQRw0bQAAAAJ) — Assistant Professor at Princeton and Chief Scientist at Together AI, creator of FlashAttention and co-creator of the Mamba state-space model.
- **Beidi Chen** (CMU) — [Google Scholar](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=jCNJhFcAAAAJ) — Assistant Professor at Carnegie Mellon and Amazon Scholar leading the InfiniAI Lab, working on efficient ML systems, sparsity, and LLM inference acceleration.
- **Ce Zhang** (University of Chicago) — [Google Scholar](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=GkXqbmMAAAAJ) — Associate Professor at the University of Chicago and former CTO of Together AI, known for ML systems, data management, and decentralized/distributed training.
- **Gennady Pekhimenko** (University of Toronto) — [Google Scholar](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=ZgqVLuMAAAAJ) — Associate Professor at the University of Toronto and Canada CIFAR AI Chair at the Vector Institute, working on computer architecture and systems for ML (benchmarking, training efficiency).
- **Aditya Akella** (UT Austin) — [Google Scholar](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=d_rxnzAAAAAJ) — Regents Chair Professor at UT Austin working on computer networking and systems, spanning cloud infrastructure, datacenter networks, and systems for ML.
- **Minjia Zhang** (UIUC) — [Google Scholar](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=98vX7S8AAAAJ) — Assistant Professor at UIUC (formerly Principal Researcher at Microsoft), known for ML systems work including DeepSpeed inference, ZeRO, and efficient training/serving.
- **Hao Zhang** (UC San Diego) — [Google Scholar](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=H1d4BS8AAAAJ) — Assistant Professor at UC San Diego and 2026 Sloan Fellow, contributor to vLLM, Alpa, Vicuna/FastChat, Chatbot Arena, and DistServe.
- **Dawn Song** (UC Berkeley) — [Google Scholar](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=84WzBlYAAAAJ) — Professor at UC Berkeley and one of the most-cited scholars in computer security, working on AI security, privacy, and trustworthy/decentralized systems.
- **Kunle Olukotun** (Stanford) — [Google Scholar](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=IzXDyR8AAAAJ) — Cadence Professor at Stanford and NAE member, a pioneer of multicore processors (Stanford Hydra) and co-founder of SambaNova Systems building reconfigurable dataflow AI hardware.
- **Christos Kozyrakis** (Stanford) — [Google Scholar](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=G2EJz5kAAAAJ) — Professor at Stanford working on computer architecture and systems, focused on datacenter/cloud efficiency, resource management, and AI infrastructure.
